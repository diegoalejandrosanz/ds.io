Music Streaming Growth
& Revenue Insights

This project focused on understanding how a frontline artist became the most-streamed artist globally that year—and how that growth translated into revenue and sustained performance for the label.

Beyond the headline results, the analysis uncovered the release and audience strategies that drove the spike in consumption. These insights helped define repeatable playbooks that could be applied to other artists in a rapidly evolving, digital-first music landscape.

We began with a year-over-year view of industry consumption to understand overall trends, volatility, and where the strongest momentum was concentrated. From there, we isolated the segments most relevant to the artist’s release cycle and performance drivers.

By comparing full-year consumption patterns against other top artists, we identified clear spikes tied to key moments in the release strategy—highlighting the scale and timing of impact. At peak, the artist accounted for 73.5% of total global streaming on a single day.

Month-over-month analysis quantified acceleration after the album release: April streams increased by +514% vs. the prior month, while listeners grew +24%, signaling strong replay value and sustained fan demand.

A key takeaway was that performance was driven primarily by core fans, with comparatively lower penetration into new audiences. This insight informed recommendations to improve top-of-funnel discovery and convert broader engagement into repeatable growth for future releases.

Citi Bike NYC Usage Analytics
Stations, Routes & Ride Splits

This project analyzed Citi Bike ride activity across New York City to identify the highest-demand stations, the most trafficked routes, and the rider segments driving usage.

The goal was to translate rider behavior into operational and marketing opportunities—improving bike rebalancing at peak stations and informing targeted messaging based on customer type and demographics.

We mapped the full station network and ranked starting locations by ride volume. One clear hotspot emerged: 46.7% of rides started at Pershing Square North, making it a critical station for availability and service levels.

Based on that concentration, a key recommendation was to prioritize faster bike rebalancing at this location to reduce shortages during peak demand and improve the rider experience.

To make route patterns easy to interpret for stakeholders, we tested multiple visualization styles (standard map, satellite view, and route overlays). This helped clearly surface high-traffic corridors and station clusters where demand was consistently concentrated.

We also segmented riders by customer type. Results showed 16.8% subscribers and 37.4% non-subscribers, with the remaining rides split between undisclosed/unknown categories. This highlighted an opportunity to reduce friction for casual riders and increase subscriber conversion.

Gender distribution was relatively balanced among riders who disclosed it (17.9% men vs. 17.7% women), with a significant portion marked as unknown or not reported—an important limitation to consider when designing demographic-based targeting.

Finally, we analyzed age tiers to understand the core audience. The data showed meaningful usage across multiple groups: 17% of riders were 50+, 22.4% were 20 or younger, and 60.6% fell between 20–50. This supported recommendations for segment-specific campaigns and messaging aligned to different rider needs.
